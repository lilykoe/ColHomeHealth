Our Specialists

Our nurses assist with Medication Pain Control IV Therapy Ostomy Care Diabetic Teaching Wound Care Case Management Resource Referral

Home Health Aides

They assist with Bathing Skin and Wound Care Exercise Programs (Supervised by a RN)

Physical Therapists

They assist patients to function independently or within the limits of a physical condition including Joint Replacement Rehab Stroke/Neuro Rehab Range of Motion Home Safety Management Gait/Balance Rehab Muscle Re-Education Pain Relief Fall Risk.

Speech Language Pathologists

They assist patients with Communication Swallowing Cognition.

Occupational Therapists

Our occupational therapists assist patients to resume activities of daily living including Dressing Preparing Meals Home Safety Management Community Re-Entry Skills.

Medical Social Workers

They assist the patient and family in planning for long term care including Financial Arrangements Guidance and Counselling

How do I receive services?

Referral

Most patients are referred by their primary physician. Referrals from other sources will be confirmed with the patientâ€™s physician, unless services are exclusively Companion services.


Qualifications

The patient must be homebound to receive Medicare home health care. The person must be unable to leave the home without the assistance of another person or a device or their medical condition makes it difficult and taxing for them to leave the home. A physician orders home health care services and supervises the plan of care. The condition must warrant the skill level of a registered nurse, or licensed therapist. Patients who are not homebound may receive home health services if they are Medicaid eligible in their own home.
